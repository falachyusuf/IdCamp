About Manokwari

Manokwari is located on the northern coast of Papua, Indonesia, and is the capital city of the West Papua Province. Known for its beautiful landscapes, Manokwari also serves as an important cultural and historical hub for the indigenous Papuan tribes.

The Manokwari region covers a vast area that includes coastal lowlands and mountainous terrains. Manokwari is known for its rich biodiversity, especially in the Arfak Mountains, which is home to various species of flora and fauna, some of which are endemic to the area. The surrounding areas include Doreri Bay and Mansinam Island, which hold significant historical value, marking the introduction of Christianity to Papua in 1855.

The Arfak Mountains are a key conservation area for rare wildlife and serve as a destination for eco-tourism, while Doreri Bay is renowned for its marine biodiversity, attracting divers and researchers alike.

Dishes

Papeda is a traditional dish from Manokwari, Papua, made from sago flour, which is derived from the trunk of the sago palm. It has a unique, gelatinous texture and is often served with a variety of side dishes such as fish in yellow broth (ikan kuah kuning) or stir-fried vegetables. The process of making papeda involves stirring the sago flour with hot water until it thickens, creating a sticky, translucent consistency.

Eating papeda is a communal activity in Manokwari, often shared with family and friends, symbolizing unity and tradition in the local Papuan culture.

Keladi Tumbuk highlights the use of indigenous ingredients, particularly taro, which is a common and important food source in Papua.
